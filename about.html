About Our Brotherhood

Six decades of discipline, valour and service to the nation.

Our Story

Founded in 1962 to prepare young boys for leadership in the Armed Forces, Sainik Military School has sent thousands of cadets to the National Defence Academy and on to the Army, Navy and Air Force. More than 80% of our ex-cadets earn commissions as officers — a record matched by few institutions.

The Old Cadets' Association keeps that brotherhood alive: mentoring aspirants through the SSB and NDA, honouring our gallantry awardees and martyrs, and standing by the families who serve. Once a cadet, always a cadet.

🎯

Our Mission

To prepare cadets — physically, mentally and morally — for entry into the National Defence Academy and a life of commissioned service, while binding old cadets in a lifelong fellowship of duty, honour and country.

🌟

Our Vision

To remain the nation's foremost cradle of military leadership — producing officers and citizens of character who lead from the front, in uniform and beyond, for generations to come.

Our Journey

  1. 1962 — The School is Raised

    Sainik Military School is founded to feed the National Defence Academy with officer material.

  2. 1971 — First Battle Honours

    Old cadets distinguish themselves in the field; the school's gallantry roll begins.

  3. 2001 — Cadet Scholarship Fund

    Old cadets launch the endowment that has since supported hundreds of deserving boys.

  4. 2015 — Old Cadets Network

    A digital register connects thousands of ex-cadets serving across the three services and the globe.

  5. 2024 — 60+ Years of Service

    Six decades on, with 4,500+ officers commissioned and the colours still flying high.
